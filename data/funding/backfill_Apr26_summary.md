## Notable themes

- **SpaceX IPO filing is generational.** April 1 confidential S-1 at $1.75T target val and ~$75B raise — biggest IPO filing in history. All four major outlets confirmed (Bloomberg, CNBC, Reuters, WSJ). Expected June 2026 listing. Repricing the entire space stack alongside the xAI February merger.
- **Amazon counter-attacks Starlink direct-to-cell.** Globalstar acquisition ($11.57B cash) gives Amazon Leo direct-to-cell capability, inheriting the Apple iPhone connectivity contract. Direct shot at SpaceX's emerging direct-to-cell business.
- **Rapidus binding milestone.** METI's ¥631.5B ($4B) approval (NOT conditional) brings cumulative Japanese government commitment to Rapidus to $16.3B. Clear separation from CHIPS PMT/DOE LPO conditional patterns we excluded — funded for 2nm production at Hokkaido IIM-1.
- **Robotics dominated by China humanoids and AV.** TARS $455M Pre-Series A (Caixin: largest single-round Pre-A in China embodied-intelligence history), DeepWay $310M Pre-IPO, Galaxea AI $290M Series B ext, X Square Robot $276M Series B, Pudu $150M = $1.48B from 5 China-domiciled companies alone. Comp set: Skild, Physical Intelligence, Apptronik.
- **Semis quantum and photonic compute concentration.** 11 Frontier Compute rounds at $3.85B (excl. Cerebras IPO and Rapidus). DustPhotonics $1.3B Credo M&A is a major silicon-photonics consolidation event. Quantum activity stays elevated (Sygaldry, SpinQ, OrangeQS, Pixel Photonics).
- **Materials thinned by post-Ascend retreat.** Ascend Elements filed Ch 11 on April 9 — directly after our 4Q23 backfill captured them at $542M Series D. Materials shrank to just 2 rows for the month: Carbon Upcycling (cement decarb $10M debt) + Renewable Metals (Australia Li recycling $12M Series A). Notable contraction signal.
- **Token sector at floor.** Cluster Protocol $5M (DePIN compute infra) is the only deal that fits. Crypto VC funding plunged ~74-75% MoM in April 2026 to ~$660M total.
- **Pre-IPO enum activated.** First use of new round value for DeepWay. Pattern will recur as more late-stage frontier-tech (esp. China) approaches Hong Kong/Nasdaq listings.
