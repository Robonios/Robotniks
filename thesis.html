Mission Directive
One Frontier Stack

Semiconductors, robotics, and space technology form a single frontier technology stack. The same chips that power autonomous robots power satellite constellations. The same automation that assembles cars assembles rockets. Tracking these sectors separately misses the dependency chain that determines which systems scale, which bottleneck, and where value concentrates.

Robotics is becoming the primary demand driver for a specific class of semiconductor: edge inference chips, motor control MCUs, sensor fusion processors, power electronics, and advanced packaging. Space systems depend on the same stack: radiation-hardened compute, autonomous navigation, robotic assembly, and satellite-grade power electronics. Semiconductors are the enabling layer without which neither robotics nor space can scale beyond prototypes.

Existing intelligence tools treat these as separate sectors. Chip-only platforms miss the demand signal. Robot-only platforms miss the supply constraint. Space-only platforms miss both. None captures the relationships that actually determine outcomes.

Robotnik exists to map the full stack — from the robot on the factory floor, to the satellite in orbit, down through their shared sensors, compute modules, chip designers, packaging houses, and foundries — because the people making the most expensive decisions in this space need to see the whole chain, not fragments of it.

This thesis guides everything
1.
Definitions: A company is in scope if its products or services are material to the frontier technology dependency chain — semiconductors, robotics, or space systems.
2.
The Robotnik Index: Tracks the full frontier stack because no existing index does. ROBO and BOTZ cover robotics but ignore semiconductors and space. SOXX and SMH cover semiconductors but ignore robotics. No index covers space infrastructure alongside its enabling technologies.
3.
Source of truth: Robotnik is the structured intelligence layer for this converging frontier stack — not just a database, not just news, not just research. The only platform that maps the full dependency chain from foundry to orbit.
4.
Market direction: As robotics and space systems move from prototypes to deployment, the semiconductor supply chain underneath becomes strategic infrastructure. Robotnik maps where this stack is strengthening, where it is brittle, and where bottlenecks and opportunities are forming.
